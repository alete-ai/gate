Eyes for an Eagle

Eagle hunter Rinat Mirlanov rests with his golden eagle above the Bokonbayevo village in Kyrgyzstan. Eagle hunting, known as berkutchi in Kyrgyz, is an ancient tradition in the country where hunters train golden eagles to hunt small prey like foxes and hares. The practice requires years of trust and reliance built between the eagle and the hunter. Although eagle hunting today is considered more of a hobby, it was originally used as a means of survival, providing both food and clothing to villagers.

Secrets of the Bees

    National Geographic Explorer Bertie Gregory explores the extraordinary lives of bees, which are among the planet’s most important animals. Over three years, special cameras opened a rare window into a single hive, revealing its hidden world. With more than 20,000 bee species pollinating one-third of the world’s food, this series uncovers their astonishing architecture and intelligence, unlocking their secrets.
